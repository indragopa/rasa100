Tributes and Remembrances

Voices of reverence for a life dedicated to vidyā, saṅgīta and dharma

Dr. R. Sathyanarayana's life touched thousands of people across generations and continents. As a guru, scholar, musician, mentor and human being, he left an indelible impression on all who knew him. On this centenary occasion, we gather voices of remembrance and gratitude from those who were shaped by his teaching, inspired by his scholarship, and moved by his devotion to music and dharma.

ajñāna timirāndhasya jñānāñjana śalākayā
cakṣur unmīlitaṃ yēna tasmai śrī guravē namaḥ Salutations to the guru who opens the eyes blinded by
the darkness of ignorance with the collyrium of knowledge

smṛti vacanaṃ

Words of Remembrance

He was not merely a musicologist in the academic sense. He was a living repository of the entire tradition, breathing its spirit into every lecture, every paper, every conversation. His knowledge of śruti was not abstract but lived; one could hear the theory become practice in his very voice. In losing him, we lost a library that no institution could ever fully replace.
A Scholar of Karnataka Music
What distinguished my father was not just the breadth of his learning, though that was extraordinary, but the depth of his commitment to truth. His approach to musicology was like his approach to chemistry: precise, rigorous, and always grounded in evidence. Yet he never lost the devotional spirit. For him, saṅgīta was both śāstra and bhakti, inseparable.
Vidwan Dr. R. S. Nandakumar, Son and Disciple
In the gurukula tradition, the guru is not just a teacher but a way of being. Dr. Sathyanarayana embodied this. His patience with students was legendary. He would explain the most complex rāga lakṣaṇa or the subtlest point of śruti theory with the same care and thoroughness, whether the listener was a senior scholar or a young student encountering the subject for the first time.
A Former Student
His Sanskrit treatises on music and dance are monuments of classical scholarship. In an age when the languages of the śāstras are in retreat, he composed new work in the traditional mode, proving that Sanskrit remains a living medium of intellectual discourse. The Puṇḍarīkamala is among the finest musicological texts produced in modern India.
A Sanskrit Scholar

ಒಬ್ಬ ಹಿರಿಯ ಸಂಗೀತಗಾರ
As President of the Indian Music Congress, he brought an intellectual seriousness and an unwavering commitment to scholarly standards that elevated the entire discourse around Indian classical music. He was equally at ease speaking at international conferences and at intimate gatherings in a Mysuru drawing room. His generosity with knowledge was boundless.
A Fellow Musicologist
The remarkable thing about Dr. Sathyanarayana was his ability to hold the scientific and the devotional in perfect balance. Here was a man who could analyse the physics of sound with the precision of a trained chemist, and in the next breath sing a kīrtana of Tyāgarāja with the transcendent devotion of a bhakta. That unity of science and spirit, of śāstra and bhakti, was the secret of his genius.
A Colleague at Sarada Vilas College
